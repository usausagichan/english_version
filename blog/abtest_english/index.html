A/B Testing - User referral Program

By CT Chen 01 January 0001

Overview

For brands, introducing new customers through existing customers can sometimes be a more efficient method than heavily investing in advertising. For example, in the case of mobile games, knowing that your friends are playing often influences your willingness to continue playing. Imagine if everyone in your class was playing a certain mobile game, wouldn’t you also be tempted to try it out? Therefore, many mobile games often launch invite-a-friend campaigns to attract you and your friends to play the game at the same time, increasing the number of new customers and the retention rate for both of you because of the mutual interest. However, if you are a planner, wouldn’t you need to test the effectiveness of the plan with a small group of players before launching the recommendation program? This is where A/B testing is needed to verify the effectiveness of the plan. This project is a practice of implementing A/B testing using data from (https://datamasked.com/)[1] to determine whether a company’s recommendation program is effective (or helpful for the company to earn more revenue).

The main task in this excercise is to perform A/B test in order to know if the referral program works(or help company to make more money) or not.

I assume the success of referral program is defined as (1) find valuable users(the referral users spent more than normal users) (2) boost the sales, or more money was spent overall.

To know whether the referral program works, we should know: (1) Is users from referral program spent more than normal users? (2) Does referral program makes the money grows up?
